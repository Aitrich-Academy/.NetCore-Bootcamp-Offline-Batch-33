About Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji is an iconic, sacred stratovolcano and the tallest peak in Japan, standing at 3,776 meters (12,388 ft). Located on the island of Honshu, it is a major cultural symbol, celebrated for its symmetrical cone It last erupted from 1707 to 1708 and is a popular destination for climbing and sightseeing. Mount Fuji is widely regarded to have an attractive volcanic cone. It has been a frequent subject of Japanese art, especially after 1600, when Edo became the capital and people saw the mountain while traveling on the Tōkaidō road.
